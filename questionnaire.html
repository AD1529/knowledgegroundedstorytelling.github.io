Story Evaluation

  1. Adherence to the controlling idea. Adherence to the controlling idea means that every part of a narrative, essay, or screenplay consistently supports and revolves around a central, guiding concept or message, often called the controlling idea. “Only by reconciling with one’s past can one find peace.”

  2. Narrative coherence. Narrative coherence refers to the logical, consistent, and meaningful alignment of all elements within a story, ensuring that events, characters, and themes fit together in a unified way that is clear to the audience.

  3. Emotional engagement. Emotional engagement is the process of creating meaningful, lasting connections by evoking strong feelings in an audience, consumer, or participant through storytelling, design, or experiences.
