Steps to Generate the Plot and Characters with Narrative Arcs

Initial Prompt

Controlling idea: Only by reconciling with one's past can one find peace.

Subject: Mechelen (Belgium), April 19, 1943. Marie and Anne, two twin sisters aged three, were traveling with their parents and their older brother Pierre on train number 20 headed to the Auschwitz concentration camp. During the journey, the train was attacked by three young members of the Belgian resistance. The girls were saved by Youra 'Georges' Livchitz, who managed to take them with him to Kiev. Pierre also survived and was placed with a Belgian family. Both their parents died during the attack. In June 1943, Livchitz was arrested. The two sisters were hidden by his fiancée's family, but on the way down to the basement, Anne tripped and suffered a cut across the palm of her hand. The wound was stitched up but left a deep scar on the palm, which greatly shocked Marie, who witnessed both the fall and the stitching. Following Livchitz's arrest, the two sisters were separated. Marie was taken in by a peasant family in Odessa (USSR). Anne was taken in by a very wealthy Jewish family in Los Angeles (USA).

October 18-22, 1964. Tokyo Olympics Artistic Gymnastics. Marie and Anne, despite their separation, share the same passion. They meet on the competition field and meet for the first time. Historical context: it's the height of the Cold War, with the conflict between the USSR and the USA. During the vault, Marie performs after Anne. Anne prepares for the test and covers her hands with chalk to ensure a firmer grip. At that moment, Marie, who is waiting behind Anne, sees the scar. The lid of the box of her memories opens.

Character Creation and Arcs

  1. Define the protagonist

    Prompt: Based on the controlling idea and the subject, create a main protagonist. Provide: name, role, key traits, central motivation, and main goal.

  2. Expand the protagonist with HKB (add HKB.zip)

    Prompt (with HKB): Using the Historical Knowledge Base, ground the protagonist in the relevant historical and cultural context for the subject. Add details about their social background, education, cultural norms, and worldview. Readapt the name based on the historical background.

  3. Expand the protagonist with PBKB (add PBKB.zip)

    Prompt (with PBKB): Using the Psychological and Behavioral Knowledge Base, expand the protagonist’s profile. Include detailed personality traits, values, fears, strengths, weaknesses, and behavioral tendencies.

  4. Define the antagonist

    Prompt: Create a main antagonist who directly opposes the protagonist. Provide: name, role, key traits, central motivation, and main goal.

  5. Expand the antagonist with HKB

    Prompt (with HBKB): Using the Historical Knowledge Base, ground the antagonist in the relevant historical and cultural context for the subject. Add details about their social background, education, cultural norms, and worldview. Readapt the name based on the historical background.

  6. Expand the antagonist with PBKB

    Prompt (with PBKB): Using the Psychological and Behavioral Knowledge Base, expand the antagonist’s profile. Include detailed personality traits, values, fears, strengths, weaknesses, and behavioral tendencies.

  7. Add supporting characters

    Prompt: Propose 2–3 supporting characters who interact with the protagonist and antagonist. For each, describe: name, role, key trait, and how they influence the protagonist’s journey or the antagonist’s plans.

  8. Generate narrative arcs

    Prompt (with PBKB + HKB): For each character (protagonist, antagonist, supporting), write a narrative arc including: initial situation, main conflict, turning point, and resolution. Ensure the arcs are consistent with the psychological traits from the PBKB and the cultural/historical context from the HKB. The protagonist’s and antagonist’s arcs must be in direct opposition, while supporting characters either enable or challenge them.

  9. Summarize in structured form

    Prompt: Summarize all characters and their arcs in a table with columns: Character, Role, Traits, Motivation, Conflict, Resolution. Make explicit how psychological grounding (PBKB) and historical grounding (HKB) influenced each arc.

Steps to Generate the Plot with Plot Points

  1. Create the high-level outline

    Prompt: Using the controlling idea, the subject, and the defined characters with their arcs, generate a high-level three-act story outline supported by the HKB.

  2. Create a scene-level breakdown (per il contest)

    Prompt: Transform the full plot structure into a scene-by-scene breakdown. For each scene, specify: setting, characters present, main action, and how it advances the inciting incident, plot points, pinch points, climax, or resolution. Ensure consistency with PBKB traits and HKB context.

  3. Refine the narrative

    Prompt: Refine the story to make it more vivid and immersive. Enrich the narrative with sensory details (sight, sound, smell, touch, taste) that bring scenes to life. Adjust the narrative rhythm by varying sentence length and pacing to create tension in dramatic moments and calm in reflective ones. Enhance the stylistic quality by improving word choice, imagery, and flow, while keeping the story coherent, believable, and consistent with the characters’ motivations and the historical context. Do not change the plot, only refine its style and atmosphere.
